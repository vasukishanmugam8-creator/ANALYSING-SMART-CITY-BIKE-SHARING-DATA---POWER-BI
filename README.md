## 📊 Project Overview
-he goal of this analysis is to visualize key performance indicators (KPIs) for a bike-sharing service. It covers station availability, peak usage times, geographic distribution to help city planners optimize bike distribution.

## 💡 Key Insights

- System Utilization: Established a baseline for system capacity and identified high-utilization hubs versus underperforming stations to pinpoint operational inefficiencies.
- Spatial Imbalances: Identified a geographic "usage gap" where urban centers experience high turnover while peripheral stations remain underused.
- Supply-Demand Mismatch: Used heatmap analysis (Station vs. Day of Week) to isolate specific stations consistently facing bike shortages, posing a risk to user reliability.
- Temporal Demand Patterns: Uncovered distinct demand cycles, identifying peak usage during commuting hours and weekends, along with significant seasonal fluctuations (higher summer vs. lower winter demand).

## 🚀 Recommendations
- Dynamic Redistribution: Implement a proactive bike rebalancing strategy during peak commuting windows to prevent shortages at "critical hub" stations.
- Seasonal Maintenance Planning: Align heavy maintenance and fleet upgrades with identified low-demand "trough" periods (winter months) to minimize service disruption.
- Targeted Expansion: Use the geographic mapping data to guide future station placements in underserved areas, ensuring equitable access across the city.
- Infrastructure Optimization: Evaluate the "Top 5" high-capacity stations for potential dock expansions and investigate "lowest availability" stations for immediate redistribution needs.
